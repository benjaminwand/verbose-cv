360째 labial pipe / Flauto mirabilis

February - April 2020

An incoming request

After not working on organ pipes since June 2019, I got a request from thingiverse, which made me research and write organ related code again, about labial pipes that have a 360째 labium. All the information is in the videos, therefore who likes text can read the text and who likes to watch me talk can watch me talk.

As far as I know, there are two companies who made such pipes so far. Kuhn calls them 'Flauto mirabilis' and builds them since 2003, and Hey built one instrument named 'Vox Maris' in 2012, here is the description on their own webpage and it was described in ISO 43.

Like I always did it when making labial pipes, I decided to use plastic pipes and only 3d-print the intricate part. 0.22 was the first file I made. It sounded surprizingly well but it was a little bit finicky to print and I found that the upcut influences the sound a lot and it was impossible to make that fixed without glueing the pipe together, which I didn't want to since I had suddenly found joy in intonation.

To fix those limitations, I wrote 0.23, where the upcut can be adjusted with a screw, you can see it in the image.

Those two models worked well given that the tube has an edge which functions as a labium. Since this isn't always given, I wrote 0.24, where the labium is printed as well.

Things I found:

  • The slit has to be thin, figure out what your 3d-printer can do. If it is too wide, you'll get no sound at all.
  • The upcut has to be low.
  • The pitch doesn't vary much with different air pressure, much less than what I knew from normal labial pipes. I was specifically looking for that because I knew that the Vox Maris has dynamic abilities.

Being an outdoor organ, the Vox Maris has to be loud, and also Kuhn builds the Flauto mirabilis as high pressure stops.

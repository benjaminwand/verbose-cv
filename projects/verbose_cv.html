Background

A list was long overdue

"Assembling a list of works" is a thing I should have been doing since a long time. Not doing it made me feel guilty in a similar way as not "learning to write code", which deserves its own article and obviously meanwhile I do write code.

But why is it so important and so charged with guilty feelings?
When introducing to people, it is common to be asked 'So, what do you do?', and this is not only a convention but for many people it seems to be inevitable knowing, they can't live without. And since I was decreasingly able to answer it throughout the years, the question became more and more horrible as well in party-like situations as in professional ones. It seems like I can either live alone on a hill and grow roses or learn to answer the dreaded 'So, what do you do?'

But why is it so hard?

In short: I do very many things and that is not how most people live and there is a lack of role models for 'doing so many things' in our nowadays western culture.
It wasn't always like that, nobody complained about Leonardo DaVinci doing many different things, and so one term for this special human operating system in 'Renaissance person', other terms are Polymath, Scanner and Multipotentialite. So yes, there are terms and self-help-literature, which is better than nothing, but it is still not well enough established in our culture that I can reply at the party 'I do many thing' and people understand and are satisfied with that answer.

Another reason why it is hard is that I'm not comfortable lying. For a while I said to tango people 'I do IT' and to IT pople 'I do tango' and it was okay-ish but do I want to be friends with people who are happy with me telling them things that are only 10% true? I don't.

In fact, I want to be seen and I want to be proud of myself. Doing many things is great, I just have ... a framing problem I guess.
I love exploring new territory and as soon as I've figured out a thing well enough I'll likely drop it and do a new shiny thing, and the 'drop it' is quite thorough, chances are that I'm so done with it that I'm annoyed with people still believing I'm the same person as when I was on the last project and referring to it all the time. So, I need to explain myself better, preferably in a way where I don't have to explain it to every single person.

And what can be won!

Part of that better communication should be being proud of that special human operating system. It is not only defence, I hope I can show off and find more likeminded people. And while doing so, I still hope that won't be forced to settle.
Still enjoy beginnings. The publication is a way to get rid of things, to be done with it, a ritual.
misunderstanding: most people are proud of finished things, I'm done with them

Strategies

I was postponing writing a list of works for years and obviously the longer I waited the more burdensome the task became. How could I make it lightweight? Non-lightweight but rewarding approaches were found in Barbara Shers 'Refuse to Choose', who preaches showing off all the things in one way or another. I wrote 'Scanner Daybook' entries for a couple of projects and found it very rewarding, and I was looking for more public ways to show all the random but significant things I'm doing.
The next idea was making a youtube video every few months and as I was collecting projects from the first quarter of 2020, I realized that that won't work either because it can't be made incrementally (on youtube it is impossible uploading a new version of a video under the same URL), and collecting a complete quarter of a year seemed a too high hurdle.
And then I published things on websites in spring 2020 and it became obvious to make the portfolio as a webpage. A webpage could be done incrementally, it has practical aspects like multimedia, links, google text search and accessibility, and it has enjoyable aspects to it too like I can learn more CSS.

Design

Since it was outdoors and without Covid-19 infection risk, I spent much time during stay-home-order on the roof top. And so it happened that I wrote the basic structure of this website with the sky as a background, and the sky made it into the design as well.
Obviously this is not a very conceptually driven design choice but since this is my first screen design I'm allowing myself to be playful. Beginning playfully is always nice.

Learnings / Outcomes

Achieving a list of works will still be a lot of work but at least I have started. I started with the Bar Chart Video and the Cotton Mask entry, which were low hanging fruits, the Productivity text was already much harder to write, but more rewarding too. The further I go back in the past the more demanding remembering and finding back information about projects. I hope this will teach me to document things when they are still fresh. But I also see that the writing itself is enjoyable, and it being demanding, like the productivity text, can be rewarding in itself.

A source of annoyance is creating open graph content. I want that my articles can be shared on social media but oh my is creating open graph content not my thing! At least I know now, I will never do that professionally.

More feedback / interaction could be nice, maybe I should share my stuff more on social media. But I'm not used to such self-promotion and it would also mean that I would have to take open graph content more seriously, oh no! :D

I very much like the visual design and I enjoy working on the visual design. Polaroids don't really work in the dark mode, I'm planning on changing them to movie posters, let's see how that goes. I also very much like the 'roofs and antennas' footer, check both light and dark mode for full enjoyment.
